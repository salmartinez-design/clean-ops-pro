## What These Screenshots Reveal

**The navigation problem is severe.** Look at what MC requires to get anywhere:

- Dashboard has a dropdown with App Dashboards AND My Dashboards — two separate lists
- Reports menu has 4 columns, 30+ links, two "View More" links that expand further
- Employees menu splits into Manage and Scheduling with 7 sub-items
- Customers menu splits into Manage, Quoting, and Other
- Company menu has Settings, Invoicing and Payroll, Other, AND Partner — 4 columns, 15+ items

To get to Payroll Summary you click: Company — then find Invoicing and Payroll column — then find Payroll Summary in a list of 12 items. That is three interactions minimum on desktop. On mobile that dropdown system completely collapses and becomes unusable.

**The dashboard is visually exhausting.** You have Weekly Jobs by day, Weekly Revenue by day, Weekly Hours by day, Weekly Hours and Jobs by day — four separate line charts with 10 color-coded service types each. An owner glancing at that on their phone sees a plate of spaghetti. No clear hierarchy, no "here is the one thing you need to know today."

**The Week in Review is actually well designed** — that page (images 9 and 10) is the cleanest thing MC has. Six KPI numbers at the top, a donut chart for job frequency breakdown, top sales people, automations count, and a payroll to revenue trend chart. That layout is simple and scannable. That is the direction our dashboard should take, not the multi-chart chaos of their main dashboard.

**Your core insight is correct.** MC was built desktop-first and never properly adapted. Their entire navigation model assumes you have a mouse, screen real estate, and time to hunt through menus. An owner checking on their business from their phone between jobs cannot use it effectively.
