You are working on CleanOps Pro, a multi-tenant SaaS platform for 
residential and commercial cleaning businesses. You are adding a 
comprehensive set of business intelligence and operational features 
designed to support cleaning companies from $200k to $5M in annual 
revenue.

TECH STACK: React 18, Tailwind, Express/Node.js, Supabase (Postgres 
+ RLS), Plus Jakarta Sans (ONLY font).

DESIGN SYSTEM — NON-NEGOTIABLE:
- Background: #F7F6F3
- Cards: #FFFFFF
- Primary text: #1A1917
- Secondary text: #6B6860
- Borders: #E5E2DC
- Accent: var(--brand) — dynamic per tenant
- NO emojis, NO dark mode, light theme only
- Plus Jakarta Sans only
- All data displays use subtle card elevation, not heavy shadows
- Status badges: pill shape, muted background, colored text

All endpoints must be protected by JWT middleware.
All queries must include company_id scoping (RLS enforced).
All features must work for both residential and commercial tenants.
Never show data across tenant boundaries.

── FEATURE 6: CLIENT CHURN RISK SCORING ──

Add churn_scores table:
{ id, company_id, customer_id, score (0-100), 
  risk_level (low/medium/high/critical), 
  signals jsonb, calculated_at }

Scoring algorithm (run weekly or on-demand):
Score starts at 0, increases with each signal:

+15 — Skipped or cancelled last cleaning
+20 — 2+ cancellations in last 60 days  
+15 — Reduced frequency in last 90 days 
      (e.g. was biweekly, now monthly)
+10 — Invoice overdue > 14 days
+20 — NPS score 0–6 (detractor)
+10 — No communication logged in 60+ days
+10 — Active since < 90 days (new client at-risk window)
-10 — NPS score 9–10 (promoter, reduces risk)
-10 — Referred another client (loyalty signal)
-5  — Paid invoice within 7 days (payment health)

Score bands:
0–25: Low risk (green)
26–50: Medium risk (yellow)
51–75: High risk (orange)
76–100: Critical risk (red)

Backend:
- POST /api/churn/calculate — run scoring for all 
  active customers in company
- GET /api/churn/scores — return all scores with 
  customer details, sorted by score desc
- GET /api/churn/scores/:customer_id — single customer score 
  with signal breakdown

Frontend:
- Churn Risk widget on main dashboard: count of 
  High + Critical customers this week
- "Retention Board" page under Customers section:
  - Kanban-style columns: Low / Medium / High / Critical
  - Each customer card shows: name, score, top 2 signals, 
    last communication date
  - Click to open customer profile
  - "Log Outreach" quick action on each card 
    (feeds communication log)
- Churn score badge on individual customer profile 
  (colored pill, score number, last calculated date)
- Weekly digest: email to owner summarizing 
  new High/Critical accounts (Resend)

── FEATURE 8: TECHNICIAN RETENTION DASHBOARD ──

Add tech_retention_snapshots table (weekly):
{ id, company_id, employee_id, snapshot_date, 
  tenure_days, jobs_completed_30d, avg_rating_30d,
  cancellations_30d, attendance_score, flight_risk_score }

Flight risk algorithm:
Score 0–100, higher = more likely to leave

+20 — Tenure < 90 days (honeymoon cliff)
+15 — No incentive earned in 60+ days
+20 — Rating trending down 2+ months
+15 — Attendance score < 80% last 30 days
+15 — Jobs completed down >25% vs prior month
+10 — No scorecard completed in 45+ days
-15 — Tenure > 365 days (loyalty signal)
-10 — Incentive earned in last 30 days
-10 — Perfect attendance last 30 days

Risk bands: same as churn (Low/Medium/High/Critical)

Backend:
- POST /api/retention/calculate — run snapshot for all 
  active employees
- GET /api/retention/scores — all employees ranked by 
  flight risk score
- GET /api/retention/:employee_id — individual history

Frontend:
- "Retention" tab under Employees section

  for clients — Low / Medium / High / Critical columns
- Each tech card: name, tenure, flight risk score, 
  top 2 signals, last scorecard date
- Click opens employee profile
- Metric summary at top: 
  "2 techs at high risk this week — 
   avg tenure of at-risk techs: 47 days"
- Dashboard widget: flight risk count (Critical + High)

── FEATURE 14: TEAM LEAD ROLE ──

Add 'team_lead' to the employee role enum 
(between technician and office).

Team Lead permissions:
- Can view + update jobs assigned to their crew
- Can clock in/out crew members (override)
- Can log communications for clients
- Can submit supply usage
- Can view (not edit) their crew's schedule
- Cannot access payroll, invoices, or company settings
- Cannot see other crews' data

Backend:

  (team lead is assigned a crew)
- Filter job queries for team_lead role 
  to crew members only

Frontend:

  My Crew (list of assigned techs + their today status)
  Schedule (crew schedule view, read-only)
  My Jobs (their own jobs)

── FEATURE 15: REVENUE FORECASTING ──

No new tables needed — computed from recurring 
schedule + historical data.

Backend:
- GET /api/forecast?months=3 — project next N months:
  { month, projected_revenue, confidence_level,
    active_recurring_clients, estimated_new_clients,
    projected_churn_losses, net_projection }
  
  Logic:
  - Base: sum of all active recurring schedules × avg invoice
  - Churn adjustment: subtract High/Critical churn risk 
    clients × their avg invoice
  - Growth: apply historical new-client-per-month rate
  - Output three scenarios: Conservative / Base / Optimistic

Frontend:
- Forecast widget on KPI Dashboard
- 3-month projection chart (bar chart, 3 scenario lines)
- Key assumptions listed below chart:
  "Based on 68 active recurring clients, 
   4.95% churn rate, 3.2 new clients/month avg"
- "What-if" toggle: slide churn rate up or down, 
   see projection change in real time
