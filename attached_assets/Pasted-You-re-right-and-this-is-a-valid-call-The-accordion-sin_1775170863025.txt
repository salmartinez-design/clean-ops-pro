You're right, and this is a valid call. The accordion/single-column layout was the wrong direction. Here's the diagnosis:

**What MaidCentral does correctly:**
- Left sidebar: compact stats + calendar — always visible, no scrolling
- Main area: **tabbed**, not stacked — one click switches context entirely
- You never scroll to find data, you tab to it

**What Qleno's current redesign got wrong:**
- Collapsed accordions sound clean but you still have to scroll to find and expand them
- A client with 3 addresses, 20 job history records, and active agreements will be hundreds of pixels tall
- On mobile it's unusable — an owner in the field shouldn't be thumbing through 40 sections to find an alarm code

**The fix — 3-panel layout with tabs:**

```
[Sticky Hero Strip — name, badges, LTV, actions]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
[Left Panel ~280px]     [Main Panel — TABBED]
Stats (always visible)  [Profile | Schedule | Billing | History | Notes]
Next job                Content for selected tab only
Quick metrics           No scroll competition between panels
```

**Tab structure:**
- **Profile** — contact info, addresses, access/entry, client notes
- **Schedule** — recurring schedule card, rate history, tech preferences
- **Billing** — payment method, invoices, QB connection, payment history
- **History** — job history table (paginated), communication log
- **More** — quotes, agreements, scorecards, portal, inspections, attachments
