This is the right instinct and the right moment to do this audit. Before a single line of code is written, every policy needs to be pressure-tested against multi-tenancy. Here is the full audit.

## The Core Problem

The PHES handbook is built around three jurisdiction-specific legal frameworks that do not exist in most other states:

**1. Illinois Paid Leave for All Workers Act (PLAWA)**
Unique to Illinois. No equivalent in Texas, Florida, Georgia, Tennessee, or most of the Sun Belt states where cleaning companies are concentrated. If this is hardcoded, every tenant outside Illinois gets a feature that is legally wrong for them.

**2. City of Chicago Minimum Wage**
Chicago has a separate minimum wage floor above Illinois state minimum, which is itself above federal. A tenant in Nashville, Phoenix, or Atlanta operates under a completely different wage floor. This cannot be a fixed number anywhere in the system.

**3. Illinois overtime framework**
Federal FLSA applies everywhere but several states layer on top — California daily overtime, for example. The system cannot assume federal-only overtime is sufficient.

## Three Things to Remove from the Software Entirely

These belong in the employee handbook document, not in the software:

1. **VESSA protections language** — This is a legal policy statement. The software should not reference specific Illinois laws by name. A tenant in Georgia has no VESSA equivalent and would be confused or misled.

2. **Equipment issuance counts** (vacuums, shirts, pants) — These are HR administrative records, not operational software data. They don't affect dispatch, payroll calculations, or any system workflow.

3. **At-will employment language** — Legal boilerplate. Belongs in the signed agreement builder (Sprint 14), not in configurable software settings.

IMPLEMENTATION RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LEGAL SAFETY RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE:
- Never reference specific laws by name in the UI
  (no "PLAWA", no "FMLA", no "AB 60" in labels)
  These appear only in helper text as examples, 
  never as enforced labels
- Never auto-populate wage floors from any data source
- Never auto-detect state or city to set minimums
- Never auto-generate termination records
- Never tell a tenant their policy is compliant
- The persistent legal disclaimer on HR Policies tab 
  must render on every page load — it is not a 
  one-time modal and cannot be dismissed

SYSTEM BEHAVIOR RULES:
- All policy fields default to blank or zero on 
  tenant creation — no assumptions
- Discipline auto-check fires after every 
  attendance log entry but produces 
  "pending review" records only — 
  owner must confirm each one
- Quality probation auto-triggers when threshold 
  is met but owner receives an alert first — 
  system does not silently change employment_type
- Leave balance activation runs as a daily 
  background check — not real-time
- All monetary values: 2 decimal places,
