**If a competitor wanted to dismantle your app**

This is a smart question to ask. Here is the honest threat landscape:

**Who they would hire:**
A penetration tester or offensive security firm — companies like Bishop Fox, NCC Group, or a freelance red team on HackerOne. Cost ranges from $5,000 for a basic web app pentest to $50,000 for a full engagement. A motivated competitor with resources could absolutely commission this.

**What they would target:**

First — your multi-tenant data isolation. The highest value attack against a SaaS is breaking the tenant boundary. If someone can query another company's clients, invoices, or employee data by manipulating API parameters, one exploit compromises every tenant on the platform. This is the most serious risk.

Second — your public endpoints. The iFrame booking widget at `/book/phes-cleaning` and all `/api/public/` routes are unauthenticated by design. A motivated attacker would hammer these with automated requests — scraping your pricing data, flooding your booking system with fake submissions, or attempting to enumerate company slugs to discover all your tenants.

Third — your Replit Secrets. If someone gained access to your Replit workspace — through a compromised account, a malicious collaborator, or a Replit platform vulnerability — they would have your database credentials, JWT secret, Square keys, QB credentials, and Stripe keys. That is total compromise.

Fourth — your JWT implementation. If the JWT secret is weak or the token validation has gaps, an attacker could forge tokens and impersonate any user including the super admin.

Fifth — your Super Admin portal at `/admin`. This is the most privileged surface in the entire platform. If the route guard has any weakness, an attacker who gets in can impersonate any tenant, access all client data, and manipulate billing.

**What safeguards are currently in place:**

From Sprint 3's security hardening we know these exist:
- Rate limiting on API routes
- Supabase RLS policies scoping all queries to company_id
- JWT authentication on protected routes
- Audit logging
- API auth audit completed
- Rate limiting on all public endpoints (30 req/min per IP)

**What is NOT confirmed and should be:**

- Whether the RLS policies have been tested for bypass — a common gap is that RLS is enabled but a misconfigured policy still allows cross-tenant reads
- Whether the Super Admin routes have a separate strong auth guard beyond JWT role check
- Whether your Replit account has two-factor authentication enabled
- Whether database backups are running automatically in Supabase
